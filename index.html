About

A different way forward

I’ve spent my career supporting people through some of life’s most difficult and tender moments. Before opening my private practice, I served for ten years as director of the Clinical Psychology Center at the University of Montana, and earlier worked as a psychologist in group practice, a range of healthcare settings, and therapeutic schools across New York, Chicago, and Montana.

I offer a warm, collaborative, and grounded approach to therapy. Rather than quick fixes, we work together to understand patterns, build emotional awareness, and make sense of your experiences in context. My work integrates evidence-based practice with deep respect for each person’s own wisdom and capacity for growth.

Starting therapy can feel vulnerable, especially if you’re used to handling things on your own. You don’t need to have it all figured out to begin. If any of this resonates, therapy can be a space to slow down, think together, and explore where you want to go next.
